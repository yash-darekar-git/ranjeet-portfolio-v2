An engineer who treats data integrity as a first principle.

01

        
Govt · ETL · Data Cleansing

        
2008 – 2009

      

      

        
Defence Accounts Pension Office, Allahabad

        
A centralized record for retired defence personnel.

        
Pension data was arriving from multiple teams in inconsistent formats, with no single, validated source of truth. The data had to be cleansed and structured before it could ever be trusted for processing.

        
Designed a normalized SQL Server schema, then built ETL workflows that imported CSV pension data and ran it through deduplication, formatting and mandatory-field validation before it ever touched production.

        
Built stored procedures, views and user-defined functions to support pension processing, plus exception reports so rejected records could be traced and fixed rather than silently dropped.

02

        
Govt · ICR · Survey Data

        
2010 – 2012

      

      

        
Government of Rajasthan · Sarva Shiksha Abhiyan & Saakshar Bharat Mission

        
Turning handwritten survey forms into state-level literacy data.

        
The state needed accurate, household-level literacy and demographic data from paper survey forms filled out across all 33 districts of Rajasthan — by field staff who had never used an ICR system before.

        
Built ABBYY FlexiCapture templates to extract handwritten and printed responses, then trained district coordinators and field staff across every district on correct form completion, before piping extracted data through SSIS into SQL Server.

        
Implemented validation rules to strip duplicates, trim formatting noise and catch invalid entries, then generated reconciliation and exception reports for state-level reporting.

06

        
Govt · Scale · ETL

        
Mission-Critical

      

      

        
Municipal Corporation of Greater Mumbai (MCGM)

        
Eighty million records, one healthy database.

        
Birth and death records arrived from multiple external vendors in Excel, CSV and text formats, all needing to land in a single, ward-wise structure spanning every administrative ward in Mumbai — without losing accuracy at scale.

        
Designed a scalable ward-wise schema with normalized tables and key relationships, then built SSIS pipelines to ingest, cleanse and de-duplicate data by registration number, name, year and ward code before it reached production.

        
Owned ongoing performance tuning, backup and disaster-recovery planning, and monitored blocking sessions, deadlocks and long-running queries to keep a mission-critical civic system healthy day to day.
